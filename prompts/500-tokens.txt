The General Electric Company (GE) was an American multinational conglomerate founded in 1892, incorporated in New York and headquartered in Boston. Until 2021, the company operated in sectors including aviation, power, renewable energy, digital industry, weapons manufacturing, and venture capital. In 2020, GE ranked as the 33rd largest firm in the United States by gross revenue on the Fortune 500. In 2023, the company was ranked 482nd on the Forbes Global 2000. In 2011, GE ranked as the 14th most profitable company on the Fortune 20, but later significantly underperformed the market as its profitability fell. Two employees of GE—Irving Langmuir (1932) and Ivar Giaever (1973)—have been awarded the Nobel Prize. On November 9, 2021, the company announced it would divide into three public companies. The new companies are focused on aviation, healthcare, and energy. The first spinoff of the healthcare division, GE HealthCare, was completed on January 4, 2023. This was followed by the spinoff of GE's energy portfolio on April 2, 2024, which became GE Vernova. Following these spinoffs, General Electric was renamed GE Aerospace, which remains the primary corporate successor and focuses on aircraft engines.
