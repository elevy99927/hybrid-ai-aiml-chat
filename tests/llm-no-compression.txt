[27/52] User: What is the Turing Test?
Bot: **The Turing Test** is a way to measure whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from a human.

## **How it works:**

A human evaluator has text conversations with two hidden participants:
- One is a **human**
- One is a **machine/computer**

If the evaluator **can't reliably tell which is which**, the machine is said to have "passed" the Turing Test.

## **Key Facts:**

**Created by:** Alan Turing, British mathematician and computer scientist

**When:** Proposed in 1950 in his paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence"

**Original question:** Instead of asking "Can machines think?", Turing asked "Can machines do what we (as thinking entities) can do?"

## **Why it matters:**

- One of the first serious proposals for testing artificial intelligence
- Shifted focus from philosophical debates to practical testing
- Still influences AI research today

## **Criticisms:**

- Some argue it only tests **imitation**, not true intelligence
- A machine could pass without actually "understanding" anything
- Doesn't test all aspects of intelligence
